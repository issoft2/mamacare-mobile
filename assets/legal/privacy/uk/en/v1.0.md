# Privacy Policy (United Kingdom)

Version: v1.0  
Effective date: 2026-05-26  
Region: United Kingdom (UK)  
Language: English

MumCare ("we", "us", "our") explains in this Privacy Policy how we collect, use, disclose, and protect personal data when you use our services.

This version is drafted for UK-focused compliance, including UK GDPR and Data Protection Act 2018 principles.

## 1. Personal Data We Collect

- Account data: name, email, phone, authentication identifiers.
- Health and profile data you enter: pregnancy status, symptom logs, care-team contacts.
- Device and usage data: app telemetry, diagnostics, device/OS metadata.
- Communications: support interactions and care coordination messages.


## 2. Purposes and Lawful Bases

We process data for:

- contract performance (service delivery),
- legitimate interests (service improvement, security),
- legal obligations,
- consent-based activities where required.

Special category data (including health data) is processed under appropriate UK GDPR Article 9 conditions and safeguards.

### 2.1. Marketing (with consent)
We may use your contact and usage data to send you updates, offers, or health tips. We only do this if you give explicit consent (opt-in). You can withdraw consent at any time in your privacy settings.

### 2.2. System Improvement (with consent)
We may use your data to improve our features, algorithms, and user experience. Where this is not essential for service delivery, we will ask for your consent. You can withdraw consent at any time.

### 2.3. Anonymous Commercialization (with consent)
We may use anonymized and aggregated data for research, analytics, or commercial purposes. This data cannot identify you. We only do this with your consent, and you can withdraw consent at any time.

### 2.4. Model Training (with consent)
We may use your data to train and improve AI models that power MumCare’s features. This is only done with your explicit consent, and you can withdraw consent at any time.

## 3. Sharing

We may share personal data with:

- processors acting on our instructions,
- care contacts you direct us to involve,
- public authorities where required by law,
- advisors under confidentiality obligations.

We do not sell personal data.

## 4. International Transfers

Where personal data is transferred outside the UK, we use recognized safeguards (for example, UK IDTA or UK Addendum mechanisms, where applicable).

## 5. Retention

We keep personal data only for as long as necessary for the purposes described, legal obligations, and claims handling.

## 6. Your UK Rights

You may have rights to:

- access,
- rectification,
- erasure,
- restriction,
- objection,
- portability,
- withdraw consent.

You also have the right to lodge a complaint with the UK Information Commissioner’s Office (ICO).

## 7. Automated Decision-Making

Where meaningful automated processing is used, we provide transparency and safeguards consistent with UK law.

## 8. Security

We use technical and organizational security controls, including encryption in transit and access management.

## 9. Updates

New policy versions may be published. Material changes may require renewed acknowledgment.
